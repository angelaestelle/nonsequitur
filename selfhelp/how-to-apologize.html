Background Info

In November 2021, we created a series of greeting cards called Remorse Code that offered a simple, friendly step-by-step, fill-in-the-blank style communication device for your relational fuck-ups. The idea was that apologizing (and real apologizing, not the half-hearted obligatory kind) is a fundamental part of healthy relationships. Honing your apologizing skills adds to your communication toolkit, and allows for you to deepen all the relationship essentials: trust, connection, vulnerability, compassion, and empathy. Below we offer the cards as a free PDF for download, in addition to further information and tips on how to use them. You can also use the cards as a self reflection tool, processing a mishap, or as a jumping point as journal prompts to apologize to yourself. We hope that this tool eases the process a little, and helps you to do the difficult (but right) thing. At the end is further reading and more resources for apology processes. Good luck!

Disclaimer (read this and think)

Remorse Code is for low-level, not (too) serious, mistakes, slip ups, and faults that we all commit and experience: forgetting a friend's birthday, not doing your half of the household chores, a passive aggressive comment towards a coworker. We all mess up from time-to-time and hurt feelings unintentionally. Not addressing things can lead to bigger problems like misunderstanding and resentment. Apologizing is HARD but it starts with taking accountability for what happened. However, for more serious harms like cheating or manipulation, you will need to take the apology beyond this card. Use your discretion and relationship history to determine your personalized apology action plan. Building a culture of accountability begins with mastering the apology, as uncomfortable as it may be. Start with yourself. Start small.

Who this is for

  • people who had or are in an interpersonal conflict involving mistakes, regrets, and slip-ups new or old
  • people who have to continue be around each other in your lives (coworkers, roommates, neighbours etc)

    • Who this is NOT for
  • those in current and past abusive situations where severe harm was done
  • people you don't have a relationship with or need/want to continue being in relationship with (set a boundary if you can)
